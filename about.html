It started on a cold rink in Nova Scotia — a kid in too-big pads, learning that the goalies who won weren’t the ones who moved fastest. They were the ones who saw it first.

Two decades later, that one idea became a method. [Founder Name] built Finngoalie around a vision-first philosophy: train the eyes before the body, and the saves follow. Along the way came the inventions the rest of the goalie world now wants — the Xtracker, a vision-training aid endorsed by pro and NHL goalies, and the patented Finnpegs P3G net-anchoring system trusted by rinks across Atlantic Canada.

From a single rink to a full camp calendar, a coaches’ convention, a certification program and a European arm — the work has always pointed the same direction: develop better goalies, at every level, by teaching them to read the game before it happens.

The Road Here

From one rink to a movement.

Every milestone came from the same loop: coach goalies, find what’s missing, build the tool, share it.

01 · Beginnings

Rink roots, Nova Scotia

A goalie’s obsession with seeing the puck early becomes a coaching idea — and a habit of building what doesn’t exist yet.

02 · First Camps

The first camps fill up

Word travels in a small hockey province. Weekly programs, clinics and private sessions grow into a full calendar.

03 · Invention

The Xtracker is born

A vision-training aid that uses visual restriction to fix tracking and stance. Pro and NHL goalies take notice.

04 · Patent

Finnpegs, patented

The P3G net-anchoring system earns a patent and becomes a fixture in rinks and dealer pro-shops.

05 · Community

East Coast Convention

A gathering of goalie coaches and a mentorship & certification program turn a method into a community.

06 · Beyond

The vision-first method — and the tech — cross the Atlantic. The crease is the same everywhere.

What we believe

Three rules. Every session.

See it first

Train the eyes before the body. Read the release, track the puck off the stick, and the save is half-made before you move.

Reps that transfer

No drill survives unless it shows up in a game. Everything we coach is built to carry from the synthetic ice to Saturday night.

Every level welcome

First-year minor goalie or AAA prospect — the philosophy scales. We meet goalies where they are and take them further.
